A handful of riddles

Collected in the Misty Mountains, Third Age

These riddles were spoken in the dark places beneath the mountains, where no sun has ever reached and no wind stirs the stagnant air. They are old, very old, and some say they were learned from the stones themselves, who remember everything.

It has no voice, yet it answers all.

It has no ears, yet it hears the smallest fall.

In dark halls it is twin to twin,

Until some light disturbs the din.

Reveal the answer

Silence — for in dark places, silence speaks back.

What has no fingers but can grasp?

What has no breath but holds you fast?

It creeps where light has never been,

And makes the bravest hearts grow thin.

Reveal the answer

The Dark — it holds all things eventually.

It swims without fins in pools without light,

No scales adorn it, yet it glows in the night.

It feeds on what falls from above,

And knows nothing of hate, nothing of love.

Reveal the answer

Time — it swims in dark waters, eating seconds and years alike, gollum, gollum.

I am born of impact, yet I have no wound.

I travel forever, yet make no sound.

In caverns of stone I live and I play,

And fade into nothing by end of day.

Reveal the answer

An Echo — we hears them still, bouncing in the deep places, repeating tales long forgotten.

I follow the fish where the sun cannot go,

I have no beginning, yet always I grow.

I cling to the swimmer, I hide in the deep,

And none who find me can find their sleep.

Reveal the answer

Shadow — it follows and grows, yes precious, it stretches long in the torchlight, reaching, always reaching...

In the deep places, riddles are more than games. They are doors that open to secrets, or walls that keep them hidden. We have spoken these ones many times, yes, in the dark when there was only the sound of dripping water and our own wheezing breath.

Some say there is no solution to the last riddle of all — the one the stones themselves whisper when the world is quiet enough. We have not heard it yet. But we listens. We always listens, gollum, gollum.

— Gollum

Return when the fire is lit and the kettle is singing.
Bilbo Gollum
